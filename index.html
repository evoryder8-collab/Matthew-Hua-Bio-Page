01  Biography

A practitioner of presence,
not generic wellness.

Matthew Hua is a Zurich-based transformational therapist and founder of healwell — an internationally awarded master of bodywork who treats the body as the fastest route to how a person feels.

His work fuses deep tissue and Asian fusion bodywork with breathwork, contrast therapy, cold exposure, heat and mindset — a single, deliberate system rather than a menu of treatments. He reads people through the body: tension, breath, posture, the held history a person carries without words.

On stage he has stood as competitor, winner, judge and the one awarding others — gold at the European Massage Championship, Champ of the Champs in Germany, gold at the Swiss Championship. The recognition matters less than what it signals: a rare command of touch, timing and the nervous system.

“He reads people through the body — and changes how a person feels, not just how a muscle feels.”

03  The Method

One system. Six disciplines.

Not a spa. Not relaxation. A deliberate sequence of pressure, breath, temperature and attention that moves a person from where they are to where they could be.

01 — Touch

Bodywork

Deep tissue and Asian fusion bodywork that locates the held tension a person can't name, and releases it with intent.

02 — Air

Breathwork

Wim Hof Method and Oxygen Advantage protocols that rewire the stress response and return command of the nervous system.

03 — Shock

Contrast therapy

Deliberate swings between states — the controlled discomfort that resets tolerance and sharpens recovery.

04 — Cold

Cold exposure

Cold as a teacher. A precise dose of stress that builds the capacity to stay calm under pressure.

05 — Heat

Heat

Heat to open, soften and surrender — the counterweight that makes the cold meaningful and the body pliable.

06 — Mind

Mindset

Autogenic training and embodied attention. Transformation holds only when the mind learns what the body already knows.
